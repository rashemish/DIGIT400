WHY DO WE NEED DATA PRIVACY?

Before answering why we need data privacy, let’s establish what data privacy is.

As the guide from Usercentrics GmbH outlines, the right an individual has to regulate how their own information (data) is accumulated, utilized, and distributed is data privacy. When this control is neglected, the outcome can be severe: identity theft or fraud for users, and regulatory, reputational or financial consequences for businesses.

As a designer, this means privacy must be integrated into the product and experience from the start, not tacked on afterward. Designers influence how consent is pursued, how data flows are represented, how defaults are set and then play a critical role in aligning user experience with regulatory demands and ethical expectations.

By embedding clarity, transparency and user-control into design, you reduce user friction, build trust, and avoid the kinds of pitfalls that turn privacy failures into product-failures.

The guide shows examples where even large platforms failed because consent was unclear, data processing vulnerable, or analyzing systems uncontrolled. I personally took this guide to heart as it really gave insight into how data privacy is practiced in the field my career happens to be in.
